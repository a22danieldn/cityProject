National capital, South Korea
Seoul , City (pop., 2010: 9,794,304), capital of South Korea since 1948, with the administrative status of a province. Located on the Han River near the centre of the Korean peninsula, Seoul was the capital of the Chosŏn dynasty (1394–1910) and the centre of Japanese rule of Korea (1910–45). During the Korean War it was the capital of the U.S. military government and suffered extensive damage; it has been largely rebuilt since 1953. In 1988 it was the site of the Summer Olympic Games. The commercial, cultural, and industrial heart of South Korea, it is a centre of higher education, with several universities including Seoul National University (1946).

Legend & mythology: Creation of the world

The most interesting Korean myth to learn would be the creation of the world. A topic that all of us have thought about at some point in our lives.

The creation myth or the founding myth states that the people on Earth didn't even exist at the start. "Yul-Ryeo," a divinity, and "Mago," a goddess, arose. Yul-Ryeo then passed away.

Gung-hee and So-hee are two deities that were born from Mago. They gave birth to two angelic men and two angelic women. Yul-Ryeo is resurrected just after the arrival of the Celestial People, and by her rebirth, the paradise and the Earth were formed, along with water, fire, and the "ki" (soul). Those components eventually created herbs, birds, plants, and animals.

The Celestial People governed all living creatures from their celestial castle, called 'Magoseong,' in honor of the deity. Mago opted to remain with Yul-Ryeo, whose entire body has become the Earth.
